§ 01

Identification

PHOTO
EXHIBIT A YP-2026-001

I am a researcher working at the intersection of cyber security, digital forensics, and robotics — focused on the systems we are beginning to trust with physical decisions.

My work asks two questions in parallel. Can we secure autonomous machines before they fail? And when they do, can we investigate them with the rigor we apply to any other crime scene? I build security architectures for robotic platforms (ROS2, humanoids, collaborative arms, aerial systems) and forensic frameworks for the inevitable incidents that follow.

I hold a Ph.D. and M.Tech. in Cyber Security & Digital Forensics from the National Forensic Sciences University, Gandhinagar, and a B.E. in Computer Engineering. My doctoral work, supervised by Dr. Parag Rughani, produced granted patents, published copyrights, and a framework for robotic incident response now used as a teaching reference at NFSU.

Before academia, I worked in industry — security advisory at Ernst & Young, and co-founding NetClinch — which keeps my research close to practice.

§ 02

Inquiry

Five lines of research I am actively pursuing.

  1. 01

    Robotic Security

    Threat modelling and proactive defense for robotic operating systems — primarily ROS / ROS2 — and physical platforms (humanoids, mobile manipulators, collaborative arms, UAVs).

    PRIMARY
  2. 02

    Robotic Forensics

    Methodologies for the forensic investigation of robots: evidence acquisition, chain-of-custody for cyber-physical systems, and post-incident analysis of autonomous behavior.

    PRIMARY
  3. 03

    Digital Forensics

    Classical disk, memory, and network forensics with applications to emerging device classes — IoT, edge, and embedded systems.

  4. 04

    Cyber Security

    Vulnerability research, secure architecture, and incident response — informed by a background in industry security advisory.

  5. 05

    Emerging Technologies

    Security and forensic implications of the next layer: AI agents, swarm robotics, ornithopter platforms, and autonomous infrastructure.
